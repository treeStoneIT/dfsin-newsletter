What is best for the client – COD or TIA?

COD vs TIA usage:

  • The Temporary Insurance Agreement (TIA) is a beneficial option for our clients as they get immediate coverage during the Life insurance application process.
  • We know that COD is a good option for some clients and for some products. For others:
    • Client not covered during a certain time frame – client at risk
    • Compensation is paid later
    • Manual tasks resulting in contract being issued twice by the insurer (indirect costs)
    • Overall, COD creates more back and forth which results in more work for all stakeholders involved

Issue:

Many clients do not have immediate insurance coverage from the time their application is submitted. Why?

  • Clients choose Cash on Delivery (COD) option;
  • Clients provide their banking information, but do not accept the TIA (Temporary Insurance Agreement).

Questions to consider :

  1. Why do many advisors uses COD vs TIA?
  2. For what reasons do clients answer «No» in the Request for TIA section, after they entered their banking information?
  3. What possible solutions could reduce the usage of COD?
  4. Is there a lack of knowledge on the usage of TIA?

53.1% of the application submitted do not take advantage of the TIA. Of the 53.1%, 29.3% of the applications had the PAD, cheque or credit card but the client answered no to answering the TIA eligibility questions.
