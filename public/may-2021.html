Habits of the Pros

When we study the habits of the highly successful professionals in our business, we discover some common habits.

1. Wake up employed. By this we mean that we wake up each day with a sufficient number of appointments set on a favorable basis, in our marketplace, which allows us to be successful in the life insurance business. When we complete a sale, we tell the client that we're going to follow up on an annual basis. "We'll go ahead and make that appointment now for (date next year)." That's one appointment that we will have next year. If we have 75 new sales this year that's going to give us 75 clients to see next year to conduct an annual review.

2. Believe in total preparation. Total preparation is so critical in the selling process. In most instances we only have one opportunity to sit down in front of a client and try to make a sale. So we must always be totally prepared to ask the right questions and to be able to answer questions and facilitate the interview so the client feels very comfortable with us and will buy from us.

3. Picture themselves as a problem-solver. By this we mean that the agent will have to explain to the client what their problems are and then show them a solution that will take care of these problems. Most people are very much underinsured and we have to share with them the reason that they need additional life insurance. View yourself as a problem solver.

4. Hone probing and listening skills. Sharpening probing and listening skills is critical in today's environment.

  1. 95% of selling is showing up.
  2. 110% of selling is showing up and being prepared.
  3. 200% of selling is showing up, being prepared and being able to ask the right questions so as to be able to be a problem solver for that client.

5. Give the buyer something to say "Yes" to. This is a great sales technique. One question to ask is: "Are you concerned about your family situation if you were not around?" Obviously they're going to say yes to this. It is always good to find a group of questions that you can ask that gives the buyer an opportunity to respond with "yes."

6. Sell what they own. You can never sell more personal life insurance to an individual than you own yourself. You will always sell personal life insurance to the amount that you own yourself. Agents can conduct a fact finder on themselves to determine if they're adequately insured. If not, they should purchase the amount of insurance that is needed to protect their family in their situation.

Keep working to develop habits that increase your professionalism and value to your clients!
