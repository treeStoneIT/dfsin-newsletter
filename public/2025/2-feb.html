Appreciation and Praise

"To get appreciation, you must show appreciation."

Showing appreciation is a mark of a true professional. Start looking for ways in which you can show genuine appreciation. You have, of course, some natural times when you should always do this:

  1. Express appreciation for new business. This is usually automatic. Be sure your clients know you appreciate doing business with them.
  2. Give appreciation to those who assist you. Try sending a note to a prospect's/client's assistant. Assistants can be most helpful to you in building a relationship.
  3. Always express appreciation for an interview. Even if a prospect doesn't buy, sincerely thanking them for granting you an interview is one of the best ways to make an impression and keep the prospect's mind open to your proposition at a future date.
  4. Show appreciation through your manners and actions. Let the buyer know where you can be reached whenever you are needed. Return telephone calls promptly.
  5. Give thanks for referrals. Always express appreciation to those customers who refer business to you. Report back to them on your results.
  6. Send prospects to your customers. If your customers are also in business, send them prospects or supply them with leads.
  7. When the subject of one of your competitors comes up, there is only one way to handle it: PRAISE YOUR COMPETITOR. This requires knowing your competition well enough to be able to praise them. Being knowledgeable about your competitors allows you to praise them. This doesn't necessarily get you a sale. However, your prospect will be more likely to accept information about your company as accurate. Never criticize your competition.

Carry this Pocket Reminder with you. Read it several times a day. Memorize it. We believe you'll beat last year's performance!

Compliance Planning

A plan to keep ahead of the known upcoming work…

As a habit you should be producing your KYC Deficiencies List monthly – and ensure that you are taking care of those that are coming up, and those that have been on the list previously. You can include future upcoming KYC’s that are expiring by entering the number of days in the Expire Time Frame box… want to know what KYC’s are also expiring in the next 30 days – enter 30 in the Expired Time Frame box and all outstanding already, and those coming up in the next 30 days will appear on the list you generate… don’t forget to change the format to Excel so you can work with the list easier…

During the KYC update process – is a great time to also ensure you have all the disclosures on file for the client – or just update all the disclosures with clients:

Trusted Contact Person – have you already completed this with the client – if yes, great – but confirm that the TCP indicated is still valid. If you have not completed the TCP – explain to the client the use and need for TCP, and then update accordingly… complete the TCP document and diarize the discussion, or just document the discussion if the client does not want to proceed currently – ensure you indicate exactly why the client does not want to proceed.

Outside Activities Form – previously known as the Dual Occupation – cannot locate it easily – then get a new one updated and send to SWO filing – Compliance is looking for these items when a new account is opened… and the auditors are looking for them when they come to visit… This was one of the most requested documents in the last two audits that were completed.

Both the TCP and OA should be completed or on file for all clients… Only one is needed per client (not per account) – so double check your files before meeting with a client or having the client sign documents.

Did your client change their email address? Please be reminded that you cannot use the new email address to update the information – a wet signature will be required, or the client must be able to access their old email to complete the update. A KYC update, changing the email address and using the new email address to e-sign will cause a Tier1 query and you will have to go back to the client.
