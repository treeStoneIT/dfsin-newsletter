Expect Great Things

“The most rewarding things you do in life are often the ones that look like they cannot be done.”- Arnold Palmer

Expect to do those things that look like they can't be done. When you set your high expectations, begin to do the little things required to reach those goals. Doing little things well is a necessary step toward doing big things with excellence.

Embrace the journey with determination and courage, knowing that each small step brings you closer to your dreams. Remember, it's not just about reaching the destination, but about the growth and learning that occur along the way. Celebrate the small victories, as they are the building blocks of your larger successes. When challenges arise, face them with resilience and creativity, understanding that obstacles are simply opportunities in disguise.

Visualize your success and let that vision drive your actions. Surround yourself with supportive people who believe in your potential, and never underestimate the power of a positive mindset. Keep pushing forward, even when the path seems arduous, for it is in these moments that your true strength and character are revealed.

Ultimately, believing in yourself and your abilities is the key to achieving the seemingly impossible. So, set your sights high, take that leap of faith, and watch as you transform the impossible into the possible.

Ask yourself these questions:

  • Do I expect great things of myself?
  • Do I expect to do the little things very well?
  • Do I expect those little things done well to pave the way to big things done with excellence?

Expect great things of yourself!

Good luck and good selling!

Sales Tip

CONDUCT A NEEDS ANALYSIS

Before presenting options to your client, conduct a needs analysis that considers exactly what they want to accomplish. This helps advisors determine which options best suit their client’s needs and clearly demonstrate why a particular choice is the best fit.

Completing a needs analysis also helps advisors remain compliant while increasing life insurance sales. Advisors who provide a needs analysis often double the face amount of policies sold compared to those who do not.

BEST PRACTICES | CHARITABLE DONATION PROGRAMS Add to quick links

Compliance November 20, 2025

Charitable giving strategies, when thoughtfully structured and aligned with a client’s broader objectives and purpose for insurance, can be a powerful and positive tool. These approaches not only support meaningful causes but also reflect the values and intentions of the client.

In some cases, charities—driven by their passion and urgency to fundraise—may reach out directly to advisors to explore opportunities. While these interactions can be well-intentioned, it is essential for advisors to remain grounded in best practices when navigating these conversations and implementing charitable strategies.

Equally important is the need to avoid any arrangement that could be perceived as trafficking in insurance policies, which is strictly prohibited. Maintaining professional integrity and regulatory compliance must always be top of mind.

To learn more about planned giving programs, visit the dedicated page. To obtain additional information and resources about planned giving, also refer to the Planned Giving page.
