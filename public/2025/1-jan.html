How to Make Your Clients Feel Appreciated

Here is what The Game Plan For Sales Success says on relationship building.

Client building involves more than discussions on existing policies and holding policy review meetings. Marketing activities targeted toward policyholders go a long way in cementing relationships and building loyalty. These ideas can be used effectively to deepen your relationship with existing clients.

  • Communicate frequently: It is important to communicate frequently with your customers. Call, email or send a card on special occasions like birthdays, anniversaries or promotions. When handing over the policy document include a company brochure highlighting other plans that may be suitable to them. Stay in touch with email newsletters, mailings, or a blog. These methods remind your clients and customers that you’re around when they need you. You can call them to let them know about new products and services, or just to ask if there’s anything you can do for them.
  • Give referrals: Link up your clients and refer business to them. Help people grow their business and they will reciprocate by telling their friends about your services. If your clients prosper you will prosper too as they will need more of your products.
  • Share the good news: Announce all your awards and accomplishments to your existing customers. When something is written about you or you are quoted in a newspaper or magazine, send copies to all your existing customers and clients. This will reinforce their confidence in you, and encourage them to recommend you to others.
  • Thank your clients: Organize a lunch, a charity or sporting event for your existing clients. Letting them know you appreciate them goes a long way in building customer loyalty. A gift certificate from a coffee chain with a note saying “The coffee’s on me” is a nice way to thank someone. Take the time to show your customers that you genuinely appreciate their business, and they’ll remember your thoughtfulness.
  • Ask for feedback: Invite feedback from your customers. Ask them if there are ways you can improve your service. When you seek feedback and take their concerns seriously, they feel a sense of ownership in what you’re doing.

Tips in planning:

  • Set your annual goals – gross commissions/settled premium/net new investments/new clients/professional development/client retention etc.
  • Break each goal into quarterly goals
  • For each quarter block for days off: weekends, statutory holidays, vacations, conference(s), pre established meetings, family time and commitments
  • This leaves the number of workdays each quarter to achieve your goals
  • Establish days that are: prospecting days/client interview days/professional development days/policy delivery days
  • Therefore a 30-day month can have:
    1. 8 weekend days,
    2. 1 statutory day
    3. 21 workdays divided into 5 prospecting days; 3 professional development days; 7 client interview days (2 or 3 each day); 5 second interview or policy delivery days (2 or 3 a day) ; 1 planning day for next month and a review of goals/achievements for the month
  • You can divide your days into categories, such as: A – only business generating days – client interviews; B days – business supporting days – i.e. prospecting, networking and professional development courses; C days – administration and planning; D days – days off
  • By concentrating your days’ activities, you become more focused, and you can better plan – i.e. Tuesdays and Thursdays are always client interview days; Mondays and Wednesdays are prospecting, networking and policy delivery days; Fridays are planning and development days (or a day off)
  • Tell colleagues or someone you trust of your plans – by sharing your goals and plans you will become accountable and motivated to honour your commitments
  • If you need to deviate from a day’s plan; adjust your schedule to replace the time that was re-allocated – especially if the time lost was a business generating day

Remember, knowing where you’re going, can make the journey easier.
