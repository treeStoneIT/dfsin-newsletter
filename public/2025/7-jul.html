Define what you CAN and CANNOT CONTROL in your life. - Kinderism

In life, distinguishing between what you can and cannot control is essential for maintaining peace and reducing stress.

What You Can Control:

  • Your Attitude: You can choose to approach situations with positivity and resilience.
  • Your Effort: You can decide how much energy and dedication you put into your tasks and goals.
  • Your Reactions: While you can't control others' actions, you can control how you respond to them.
  • Your Choices: You have the power to make decisions that align with your values and aspirations.
  • Your Actions: You can take proactive steps towards achieving your goals and improving your life.

What You Cannot Control:

  • Other People's Actions: You can't dictate how others behave or the choices they make.
  • The Past: What has already happened cannot be changed, only learned from.
  • The Future: While the future is uncertain, planning and preparation helps alleviate the lack of control.
  • External Events: Natural disasters, economic shifts, and societal changes are beyond your control.
  • Others' Opinions: You cannot control how people perceive you or what they think.

By focusing on what you can control, you empower yourself to live a more intentional and fulfilling life, while letting go of what you cannot control helps cultivate acceptance and peace.

Take a step back, reflect, and define what you can and cannot control in your life. Identify the things that are within your power to change, and those that are beyond your influence. Recognize the areas where you need to focus your energy and attention, and let go of the things that are outside of your control.

  • According to a LIMRA Canadian Life Insurance Barometer Study, when asked, ‘who needs it most?’ Canadians making under $50K per household (38% of people surveyed), as well as those making between $50K and $150K per household (28% of those surveyed) had a need for life insurance. This means that 2/3 of Canadians below the $150K family income threshold indicated a need for life insurance. It serves to note that Gen Z had the greatest need (likely due to the greater prevalence of liabilities at that age).Working class Gen Z is between 18-28 years of age. Further, 28% of men and 32% of women, when asked, needed coverage.
  • Did you know that when asked, Canadians said the biggest reason that they don’t want life insurance is that they think it’s too expensive. Following this up with a question about what they perceive the cost to be, most surveyed overestimated the cost of insurance by 3X.

Compliance June 26, 2025 - Compliance – FSRA Business Practice compliance questionnaire

The Financial Services Regulatory Authority of Ontario (FSRA) will soon begin administering the Business practice compliance questionnaire to advisors who hold an Ontario life insurance license. The questionnaire is essentially an advisor audit. It will be administered electronically, which means it could conceivably be sent to hundreds or thousands of advisors. The questionnaire is similar to Advisor practice reviews, which are administered by insurance companies. Please refer to the connect bulletin (June 26th) to learn what you must know and what you should do.
