Do You Procrastinate?

“Procrastination is caused by our perception that doing a certain task will cause us pain. We tend to avoid events that are unpleasant, complex, lengthy, or uninteresting, regardless of their priority.”- John C. Maxwell

Do you have the tendency to procrastinate? If you do, you are a person who has mastered the art of keeping up with yesterday.

Take a good, hard, honest, daily look at yourself to determine if you have slipped into the habit of putting things off until tomorrow.

Write it on your heart: Success is the product of today, today's responsibilities fulfilled, today's opportunities seized, today's jobs attended to, today's sales finalized.

Tomorrow never really comes. Every morning when the sun rises, it's today! That's when sales are made. That's when the all-important signatures are written on the dotted line.

There's a wise old saying we should listen to: One of these days is none of these days. Some salespeople get ready and then do something. Many just keep getting ready!

Do you ever promise yourself that tomorrow will be better? It can be, simply by making today the best day possible. To make tomorrow a great day, start today!

Promise yourself your work will be done today, the unpleasant task will be cleared up today. a great day, start today!

Today you owe it to yourself, your family and your organization to break loose from the bonds of procrastination and leap into the exciting world of today's accomplishments, present achievements, and immediate rewards.

Do it now!

Good luck and good selling.

Compliance

Compliance February 12, 2026

When completing electronic applications for life insurance or investment products, please take special care to ensure you’re licensed in the province the client is occupying at the time of click-signature. The insurer application system will immediately detect the province the client “signed at” and it will detect the provinces of licence embedded in your advisor code with that carrier.

Executing an “out-of-province” application where you’re not licensed may result in the insurer immediately reporting you to the regulators in all provinces in which you are licensed. This may result in disciplinary action by the regulator and often results in the regulator(s) auditing your practice and client files.

Before faulting insurers for being too strict and punitive in this regard, know that technically they do have a duty to report. Even before clicking Submit on an e-application for a client who was not in the advisor’s province of licence, the advisor was probably giving advice to that client days or weeks prior to completing the e-application. That is the infraction. The submitted e-application was just the tip-off that made the insurer realize that the advisor was giving advice in a jurisdiction in which they were unlicensed.

Contact your manager or compliance officer if you have any questions.

Mutual Funds Dealer – February 23, 2026

Quick link

With the transition to the new dealer scheduled for next May, you must make an important decision regarding the handling of your segregated fund accounts if you hold both mutual fund and insurance licences.

Insurance-only licence

If you hold an insurance licence only, no action is required.

On June 19, at 4 p.m. (EDT), segregated fund accounts will be automatically transferred to the Wealthserv platform managed by IDC.

Mutual fund and insurance licences

If you hold both a mutual fund licence and an insurance licence, you must choose between 2 models:

  1. MGA model – administered by IDC (Wealthserv platform)
  2. Investment dealer model – with the new DFSI dealer (Dataphile platform)

For those who choose the MGA model, the transfer of the segregated fund accounts to Wealthserv (IDC) will take place on June 19 at 4 p.m. (EDT).

For those who choose the investment dealer model (Dataphile), the transfer will occur on May 9 or 10, during the conversion.

If no choice is made, the investment dealer model will be selected by default.

You can consult the comparison table below to review the impacts for you and your clients.

Procedure to confirm your choice

If you hold both licences (insurance and mutual funds), it is essential that you submit your choice no later than March 23. To do so, please inform your Financial Centre of your selection.

New Helios2 Contract purchases ending March 31, 2026

In September, Desjardins Financial Security Life Assurance Company (DFS) announced its decision to stop opening new contracts for Helios2.

As of March 31, 2026, at 4:00 p.m. ET, DFS will no longer accept any contract applications received for Helios2 contracts.

This decision is part of a broader strategic initiative aimed at simplifying and optimizing investment products (mutual funds, structured products, exchange-traded funds, term investments, and annuities) while meeting the current and future needs of members and clients.

What you need to know

  • This measure only applies to new sales.
  • Clients who already have a Helios2 contract will not be affected. They will retain all their rights and benefits, and the administration of existing contracts will continue normally until maturity.
