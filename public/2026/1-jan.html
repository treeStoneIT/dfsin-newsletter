Take Responsibility for Your Success

Resolve to spend the bulk of your time in green-time activities - setting appointments and conducting selling interviews with potential customers and current clients.

Most of what we do in sales is beyond our control. Our customers make the rules. Our job is to show up and do our best.

The nature of the beast is that any number of a thousand things can kill a sale (sometimes two thousand).

When asked what went wrong or why we failed to get the business, we have to train ourselves to make the shortest answer possible. Excuses, no matter how valid, are not the business we are in. Our business is sales, and that means moving on to the next call without looking back. The more calls we make, the more sales we make.

We know that the clock only moves in one direction -- so should we. Fast forward is what we are paid for. Push that button in your head and get on with the day. The more places you can get to each day, the more success you will enjoy.

Take responsibility for results and block excuses out of your mind. Correct any mistakes you made and move forward! Keep working hard. Honest, intelligent effort is always rewarded. Hard work is the key to success.

Ted Talk

Hi All,

Hope this finds you well heading into the holiday season. I think it’s worth noting this new trending report of sales and what product “type” most of those premiums go towards. UL and term are consistent within a range and tend not to move the market trending. Whole life, as represented below, shows the most variability of premium in sales. A large whole life premium number is indicative of advisors in Canada generally moving “up scale” or converting some of those term insurance sales into whole life premiums.

It should be obvious that annualized premiums are significantly skewed towards Whole Life (generally Participating insurance), whereas face amount and policy count are highest in Term insurance. Most notable on the slide below is that whole life policies are on an upswing and outpace UL sales by almost double, a sign of the times perhaps where people are unsure about the financial outlook.
