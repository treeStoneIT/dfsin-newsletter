Sell Courageously!

“Compared to what we ought to be, we are only half awake. The average individual performs far below potential. We possess power of various sorts that we habitually fail to use.”- William James

After Dr. David Livingstone had begun his now famous missionary work in Africa, a certain group wrote the great pioneer and asked: "Have you found a good road to where you are? If so, please advise. We'd like to send others to assist you."

Dr. Livingstone sent this reply: "If you have those who will come only if they know there is a good road, I don't want them. I want individuals who are strong and courageous -- those who will come if there is no road at all!"

What a lesson in courage - especially for salespeople!

Courage is performing the task that would be easier not to do. Courage is accepting the responsibilities when it would be more comfortable not to accept. It's moving out on faith, when you know full well the risks and the pitfalls that will challenge you. It's marching ahead, blazing a new trail where there are no roads, and sometimes no maps to guide you.

Remember these truths:

"Courage is knowing what not to fear." Socrates

"One man with courage makes a majority." Andrew Jackson

Sell courageously!

Ted Talk

I recently read a LIMRA article on insurance in younger demographics. I felt that there were a few items that were worth noting.

"My advice to life insurance companies would be to focus on simplicity, transparency, and personalization. Make the process easier to understand and by offering clear explanations and avoiding jargon, especially when explaining policy terms and pricing. Personalize the experience by providing tailored coverage recommendations based on individual needs, life stage, and budget. Finally, prioritize building trust through honest, informative content and a focus on long-term customer relationships rather than just mak[ing] a sale." — Patrick, owner, age 32

We're all aware that the monumental shift in the industry from 'sales people' to providing valuable advise is long in the past, but as I read quotes like the one above, I'm still left thinking that occasionally our advisors overthink and over 'talk' the process. Young people have a lot of misconceptions:

We all know that younger generations tend to get their information online…in fact 29% of young people looked for financial product information advice online in 2019. That number has exploded to 62% in 2025 according to LIMRA. Younger clients are coming ready and equipped with basic knowledge to appointments now, so you should be gauging how much information they actually need by product, but more importantly, figuring out the NEED, which gains the trust we need to move ahead.

Social media connects people to dynamic communities discussing the pros and cons of life insurance, which can spark interactions that lead to policy purchases. Some participants follow specific hashtags or financial influencers to benefit from the value they provide.

I've seen some of our advisors online and have very straight forward videos identifying some of the common misconceptions of life insurance. With 61% of young people vastly overestimating the cost of insurance and 35% feeling like they know enough about life insurance in general, needless to say we have a lot more educating to do.

I'll leave this which is being asked of companies. By proxy…you can get ahead of things like this:

RBC Medical Student Offer (MSO)

The RBC Medical Student Offer (MSO) provides comprehensive disability insurance and financial protection tailored for medical students, residents, and fellows in Canada.

Overview of the RBC MSO:

The RBC Medical Student Offer is designed to help medical students secure disability insurance at a lower cost while providing essential coverage during their training and early career. This offer includes several key benefits:

  • Disability Insurance: The MSO allows medical students to purchase disability insurance with industry-leading limits, ensuring financial protection in case of illness or injury that prevents them from working. Coverage can be up to $25,000 per month.
  • Discounts and Free Coverage: The offer includes various discounts and free coverage options: 10% discount with up to 12 months free coverage, or 25% discount with up to 5 months free coverage for medical students, residents, and fellows.
  • Simplified Application Process: The application process is straightforward, often requiring just one page, and may not necessitate a medical exam, making it accessible for students.
  • Future Income Option (FIO): This feature allows students to increase their coverage annually without needing to provide medical evidence, up to a maximum of $25,000 tax-free.
  • Guaranteed Premiums: The premiums and contract provisions are guaranteed for the life of the contract, providing stability and peace of mind against market fluctuations.

For more details or to apply, you can visit the RBC Insurance website.
