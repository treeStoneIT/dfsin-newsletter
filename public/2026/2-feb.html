Excellence is Always Noticed

“The secret of success is doing ordinary work extraordinarily well.”- John D. Rockefeller, Jr.

There is a quiet but powerful advantage that comes to professionals who deliberately put the mark of quality on everything they do. The habit of working accurately, carefully, and completely shapes not only results, but character. The effects are gradual and subtle, yet profound. Each time you choose to do the right thing you earn an inner approval that builds confidence and pride.

On the other hand, rushed, careless, or half-finished work leaves behind a trace of erosion. Over time, those small compromises weaken standards, and excellence becomes harder to achieve.

There is no stronger marketing tool for a sales professional than a solid reputation. The world’s most respected brands treat their names as priceless assets. They know that integrity, reliability, and excellence are worth protecting at all costs. A trusted name carries value because it consistently delivers quality.

Never settle for “good enough,” “close enough,” or “that should work.” Finish every sales task completely and insist on your best effort every time. Someone is always watching. Someone will notice. And that someone may become your next long-term customer.

Holding yourself to a high standard gives your work real power. Selling, when practiced professionally, teaches an important truth: you are not paid for who you know or even what you know. You are paid for how well you serve others using what you know.

The greatest gift we can offer the world is ourselves at our best. Whatever your role, whatever the task give it your best. Put your signature on it. Stamp it with excellence.

Good luck and good selling.

Compliance January 22, 2026

The Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA) and Canadian Investment Regulatory Organization (CIRO) have issued a Joint CSA/CIRO Staff Notice 31-368 – Client Focused Reforms: Review of Registrants' Know Your Client, Know Your Product and Suitability Determination Practices and Additional Guidance.

This notice summarizes findings from compliance reviews of 105 firms and provides guidance on meeting Client Focused Reforms (CFR) requirements. The key takeaway: robust documentation is essential to demonstrate compliance.

Key Guidance for Advisors

Know Your Client

  • Assess risk tolerance (RT) and risk capacity (RC) separately, and use the lower of the two unless documented otherwise
  • Collect detailed financial information, and follow up on unclear or inaccurate data
  • Keep KYC information current following significant changes, record meaningful updates and document efforts made to contact unresponsive clients

Know your Product

  • Understand all recommended or traded securities, including underlying holdings in model portfolios
  • Assess and document securities transferred in or from client-directed trades

Suitability

  • Consider all suitability factors and document the rationale
  • Monitor concentration and liquidity across accounts, and document exceptions
  • Evaluate costs and compare alternatives, and justify higher-cost recommendations
  • Conduct meaningful reassessments at required intervals or when triggering events occur
  • Keep in mind that client-directed trades still require suitability assessment

Ted Talk

In 2021, about 8.3M people were considered landed immigrants to permanent residents. There's no indication of this slowing down. In fact, by 2041 as much as ½ of Canada's population will be made of landed immigrants or permanent residents. While companies that do multi-level marketing to sell life insurance are often seen in a certain light, if we don't adapt to this ever changing growth market, we will lose significant market share to these companies, as landed immigrants have an early tendency to work with people they know, from their home country. Something to consider is perhaps finding a strategy that works for you to tap into some of these important markets.

Family Dynamics and Structures Are Changing

  • Canadians getting married later and having fewer children (below replacement rate)
